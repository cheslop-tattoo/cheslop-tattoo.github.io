How do I book an appointment with you?

You can book an appointment through my online booking form by clicking the "Book a Tattoo" button in the navigation menu. Please provide detailed information about your desired design, size, and placement to help me prepare for your session.

How far in advance are you booked up?

I typically book 2-3 months in advance. However, this can vary depending on the season and the complexity of the design. I recommend booking early to secure your preferred date.

Can I walk in, or do I need an appointment?

I work by appointment only to ensure I can give each client my full attention and prepare properly for their design. This helps maintain the quality of work and ensures a comfortable experience for everyone.

Do you offer consultations?

Yes, I offer free consultations for custom designs. This gives us a chance to discuss your ideas, review reference images, and ensure we're on the same page before your appointment.

How much does a tattoo cost?

My minimum price is ¬£80 for small, simple designs. Larger or more complex pieces are priced based on size, detail, and time required. I'll provide a quote after reviewing your design request.

Can you copy a tattoo I found online?

I don't copy other artists' work directly, as this would be copyright infringement. However, I'm happy to use it as inspiration to create a unique design that captures the elements you love while being original.

Can I bring my own design?

Absolutely! I welcome client designs and can help refine them to work well as a tattoo. I'll review your design and suggest any necessary adjustments to ensure it translates well to skin.

Can I see the design before the appointment?

Yes, I'll share the design with you before your appointment. This allows time for any adjustments to ensure you're completely happy with the design before we start.

How long will the tattoo take?

The duration depends on the size and complexity of the design. Small pieces might take 1-2 hours, while larger pieces could take multiple sessions. I'll give you a time estimate when you book.

What's your cancellation or reschedule policy?

I require 48 hours notice for cancellations or rescheduling. Late cancellations may result in a deposit being charged for future bookings. This helps me manage my schedule and accommodate other clients.

How should I prepare for my tattoo appointment?

Get a good night's sleep, eat a substantial meal before your appointment, and stay hydrated. Avoid alcohol 24 hours before your appointment. Make sure the area to be tattooed is clean and well-rested.

What should I wear to my tattoo session?

Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that allows easy access to the area being tattooed. Dark colors are recommended as they're less likely to show ink stains.

Should I shave before my appointment or will the artist do it?

I'll handle the shaving as part of the preparation process. This ensures the area is properly prepared and reduces the risk of irritation.

Can I bring a friend with me to the studio?

Yes, you can bring one friend for support. However, please note that space is limited, and they should be prepared to wait quietly during the session.

Do I need to eat before getting tattooed?

Yes, it's very important to eat a substantial meal before your appointment. This helps maintain your blood sugar levels and makes the experience more comfortable.

Can I drink alcohol the night before my appointment?

No, please avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours before your appointment. Alcohol thins the blood and can affect the tattooing process and healing.

Can you tattoo over scars or stretch marks?

Yes, I can tattoo over scars and stretch marks, but it depends on their age and condition. I'll assess the area during the consultation to determine the best approach.

Can I get tattooed if I'm pregnant or breastfeeding?

No, I don't tattoo pregnant or breastfeeding individuals as it could pose health risks. Please wait until after you've finished breastfeeding to get tattooed.

Can I get tattooed if I have a disability or medical condition?

Please let me know about any disabilities or medical conditions during booking. I'll work with you to ensure a comfortable experience and may request a doctor's note for certain conditions.

Can I get tattooed if I'm feeling unwell or have a cold?

No, please reschedule if you're feeling unwell. Getting tattooed while sick can affect your immune system and the healing process.

Can I get tattooed if I'm under 18 with parental consent?

No, I only tattoo clients who are 18 years or older, regardless of parental consent. This is for legal and ethical reasons.

Can I get tattooed if I have allergies or sensitive skin?

Please inform me of any allergies or skin conditions during booking. I use hypoallergenic inks and can perform a patch test if needed.

How do I take care of my new tattoo?

I'll provide detailed aftercare instructions after your appointment. Generally, you'll need to keep the tattoo clean, moisturized, and out of direct sunlight. Avoid swimming, soaking, and picking at the tattoo during healing.

Is it normal for my tattoo to scab or peel?

Yes, scabbing and peeling are normal parts of the healing process. However, if you notice excessive scabbing or unusual symptoms, please contact me immediately.

What should I avoid while my tattoo is healing?

Avoid swimming, sun exposure, tight clothing, and picking at the tattoo. Don't apply any products not recommended by me, and keep the area clean and moisturized.

Can I go swimming after getting a tattoo?

No, you should avoid swimming for at least 2 weeks after getting tattooed. This includes pools, oceans, and hot tubs to prevent infection and color loss.

When can I work out after getting tattooed?

Wait at least 48 hours before light exercise, and avoid activities that cause excessive sweating or friction on the tattooed area. Full workouts can resume after 2 weeks.

Do I need a touch-up?

Most tattoos heal well without touch-ups, but I offer one free touch-up within 3 months if needed. This ensures your tattoo looks its best for years to come.
